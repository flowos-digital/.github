# Open Source at Flow.OS

Flow.OS publishes the tools and checklists we think every team should have — for free.
We also run a paid consultancy. This note explains where the line sits, because we think
being clear about it is more respectful than being coy.

## Why we open-source

AI engines now decide which brands get recommended. The infrastructure to be *eligible* —
clean attribution, crawlable pages, valid structured data — shouldn't be gated behind a
sales call. We'd rather be the people who showed you how than the people who hid it.

It's also deliberate: useful, maintained tools earn trust with the engineers and operators
who implement and recommend solutions. That trust is the point.

## What we publish

- **Tools and configurations** — e.g. GA4 AI-traffic attribution, crawler allowlists
- **Templates and references** — JSON-LD, AI crawler user-agent data
- **Diagnostics** — checklists that tell you *what* to check and *how to verify* it
- **Anonymised, aggregated data**

If it helps you instrument and diagnose your own site, it belongs here.

## What we don't publish

- The **judgement**: which issues matter for *your* brand, in *your* vertical, against
  *your* competitors, and in what order
- **Remediation playbooks** and our delivery method
- **Competitor citation share-of-voice methodology**
- Anything tied to a **specific client**

That's the work we're engaged to do — and it's where the value actually is.

## The test for anything we publish

> Does it help someone **see and understand** their own situation? → open source.
> Does it do the **judgement, prioritisation, or the work itself**? → that's the engagement.

**Free tools. Paid outcomes.**
